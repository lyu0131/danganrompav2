Game Play

The Premise

You are Makoto Naegi, a student trapped in Hope's Peak Academy. To escape, you must survive a twisted game of mutual killing orchestrated by the sadistic bear, Monokuma. Find the truth. Expose the lies. Fire the bullet.

School Life

Explore the academy in first-person, gather clues, and build relationships during Free Time. Give presents, unlock character backstories, and earn powerful Skills for trials.

Class Trial

When a body is discovered, debate your classmates in high-stakes trials. Your Influence is your lifeline — make too many mistakes and it's game over.

Trial Mechanics

Nonstop Debate

Arm yourself with Truth Bullets and shoot down lies in real-time debates. Find contradictions, silence disruptive chatter, and break the cycle.

Hangman's Gambit

Shoot down letters to spell out crucial clues in this fast-paced puzzle minigame.

Bullet Time Battle

Face off one-on-one in rhythm-style showdowns. Lock onto remarks and fire to the beat!

Closing Argument

Piece together a comic strip depicting exactly how the crime unfolded.

Bonus: School Mode

Unlocked after clearing the game! Build backup Monokumas, manage resources, and explore character relationships in this alternative dating sim-style mode.

About

Hope and despair clash in bold typography and neon shadows. This fan project rebuilds Trigger Happy Havoc as an interactive stage, where every hover, flash, and UI snap echoes the rhythm of a Class Trial. The site aims to capture the game's signature atmosphere — playful menace, theatrical mystery, and the relentless push toward truth.

The design leans into the game's visual language:
the crosshair that never stops searching,
the Truth Bullets loaded with contradictions,
and the pulsing pink-on-black world where hope fights to stay alive.

Nothing here is official — only a tribute crafted with care, admiration, and obsessive attention to Danganronpa's distinctive style. This is a space to revisit the tension, the humor, and the unforgettable characters through a modern, fan-created lens.
